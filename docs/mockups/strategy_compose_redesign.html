Execution Profiles / Aggressive Market In/Out

Aggressive Market In/Out

How orders are sent and managed at the broker. The profile knows nothing about legs, stops, or targets — those all live on the strategy. The profile only owns entry expression, bracket placement, and runner mechanics.

Entry expression

Bracket placement

Where the bracket lives. The strategy defines the legs themselves; this just decides who manages them.
Native: send the full bracket to the broker (Alpaca OTOCO etc.). Synthetic: engine watches price and places exits as triggers hit.

Runner mechanic

If a strategy has a runner leg, this is how the trail is implemented. The strategy specifies the trail amount; this decides who moves the order.
Native trailing is more reliable but only supports a single offset. Engine-managed lets you trail by ATR or other features.

Slippage & guards

What this profile owns

  • Entry order type · TIF
  • Native vs synthetic bracket
  • Runner trail engine (broker-native vs engine-managed)
  • Slippage / spread / liquidity guards

What it does not own

  • Number of legs
  • Stop / target levels (per leg)
  • Trail amount
  • Stop-handling on fill
All of that lives on the strategy.

Why this is safe

  • Bindings are version pointers on the deployment row — not edits to the libraries themselves.
  • Open positions stay on the old binding until exit (they were placed under those rules).
  • "Effective tomorrow" delays the swap to the next session boundary so today's tape is consistent.
  • Every swap is logged with timestamp + actor.
